This section lists and briefly introduces the blogs that have been published on the [AWS Study Group](https://www.facebook.com/groups/awsstudygroupfcj). The following are examples:

### [Blog 1 - LEARNING ABOUT AI TRAFFIC ANALYSIS DASHBOARDS IN AWS WAF](3.1-Blog1/)

This blog introduces the new **AI Traffic Analysis Dashboards** feature in AWS WAF, which enables operations teams to monitor and analyze traffic generated by AI systems (AI crawlers and AI agents) separately from traditional bot traffic. The dashboard identifies which AI crawlers are accessing the application, measures their traffic volume, and highlights the URLs or endpoints receiving the most AI traffic, providing organizations with better visibility into AI-generated traffic.

### [Blog 2 - AWS SHIELD ADVANCED ATTACK FLOW LOGS FOR DDoS ATTACK MONITORING](3.2-Blog2/)

This blog introduces the **Attack Flow Logs** feature of AWS Shield Advanced, which records detailed traffic information during DDoS attacks, including source and destination IP addresses, network protocols, countries of origin, and mitigation actions. The collected data can be exported to Amazon S3, Amazon CloudWatch Logs, or Amazon Data Firehose for advanced monitoring, incident investigation, and security analysis.

### [Blog 3 - REFLECTING ON A MODERN ARCHITECTURE FOR KYC MODERNIZATION USING SERVERLESS & AGENTIC AI ON AWS](3.3-Blog3/)

This blog reviews an AWS and IBM whitepaper on modernizing Know Your Customer (KYC) processes using Agentic AI, event-driven architecture with Amazon MSK, and Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) integrated with a knowledge base. Besides highlighting the strengths of the proposed architecture, the blog also discusses several important concerns, including the lack of experimental validation, legal accountability for AI-assisted decisions, and emerging security threats such as deepfake attacks and synthetic identity fraud.
