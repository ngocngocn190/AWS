# LEARNING ABOUT AI TRAFFIC ANALYSIS DASHBOARDS IN AWS WAF

AWS WAF has recently introduced a new feature called **AI Traffic Analysis Dashboards**, which enables security and operations teams to monitor and analyze traffic generated by AI systems (AI crawlers and AI agents) separately from traditional bot traffic. This feature addresses the growing trend of AI crawlers—such as those used by AI chatbots and AI-powered search engines—accessing websites to collect information or support retrieval tasks. Although these requests are not necessarily malicious, they still consume system resources and should be monitored independently.

## Key Takeaways

* The dashboard identifies which AI systems are accessing an application (such as crawlers from OpenAI, Anthropic, Perplexity, and others), rather than simply displaying the total number of requests.

* AI bot identification is not based solely on the User-Agent header, which can be spoofed. AWS WAF Bot Control also uses additional verification mechanisms to improve the accuracy of AI bot identification.

* The dashboard provides visibility into which URLs or endpoints (such as blogs, technical documentation, APIs, or product pages) receive the highest volume of AI traffic. This helps administrators determine where AI traffic is concentrated and apply appropriate rate limiting or caching strategies.

* It allows organizations to monitor AI traffic trends over time, including traffic increases or decreases, the most active AI bots, and periods of unusual activity.

* The purpose of this feature is not to block AI traffic, but to provide organizations with greater visibility and transparency into how AI systems interact with their web applications.

## Personal Reflection

This feature demonstrates that modern cloud security services are beginning to recognize AI-generated traffic as a distinct category that requires independent monitoring, rather than grouping it together with malicious bots or spam traffic. As AI agents continue to play a larger role in web data collection and information retrieval, organizations need better visibility into this new type of traffic to optimize performance, improve security, and make informed traffic management decisions.
